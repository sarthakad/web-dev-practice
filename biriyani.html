Recipe For Chicken Biriyani

Biryani is a mixed rice dish originating among the Muslims of the Indian subcontinent. It is made with
Indian spices, rice, and usually some type of meat or in some cases without any meat, and sometimes, in
addition, eggs and potatoes.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into medium-sized pieces)
  • 3 cups basmati rice
  • 2 onions (sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 2 tablespoon garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 2 teaspoons cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste

    • Instructions:

    1. Soak the basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and keep aside.
    2. Heat the ghee and oil in a large pot. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
    3. Add the chicken pieces and fry on high heat for 5-6 minutes, or until they turn brown.
    4. Add the ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, chopped tomatoes, cumin seeds, coriander powder,
      red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 5-6 minutes or until the tomatoes are soft.
    5. Add the yogurt, mint leaves, and coriander leaves. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes.
    6. Add 2 cups of water and pressure cook the chicken for 10-12 minutes, or until it is tender.
    7. Meanwhile, boil 4 cups of water in a separate pot. Add the soaked basmati rice, bay leaves, green
      cardamoms, cloves, cinnamon stick, and salt. Cook until the rice is almost done.
    8. Drain the water from the rice and remove the bay leaves, green cardamoms, cloves, and cinnamon stick.
    9. Spread a layer of cooked rice over the chicken in the pot. Repeat the layering process with the remaining
      rice, making sure to end with a layer of rice on top.
    10. Pour 1 cup of water over the rice and cover the pot with a lid. Cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes, or until
      the rice is fully cooked and the water has been absorbed.
    11. Turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for 10-15 minutes.
    12. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with raita or any other side dish of your choice.
      13. Enjoy your delicious chicken biryani!
